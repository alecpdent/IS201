What is disc golf?

"Disc golf is played much like golf except, instead of a ball and clubs, players use a flying disc. The sport was formalized in the 1970s and shares with golf the object of completing each hole in the fewest strokes (or, in the case of disc golf, fewest throws). A disc golf hole begins from a tee area and ends at a target, the most common of which is an elevated metal basket." (Source: https://www.pdga.com/)

Disc golf in Utah

Utah has many great place to play disc golf. In 2024, UDisc (The app for disc golfers) named the following 5 courses as the top courses in Utah.

  1. The Fort
    • Location: Ogden, UT
    • Year Established: 2018
    • Special Note: Home of the PDGA Disc Golf World Championship, 2021.
    • See this course on UDisc: The Fort
  2. Base Camp Adventure
    • Location: Moab, UT
    • Year Established: 2004
    • Special Note: 4WD recommended.
    • See this course on UDisc: Base Camp Adventure
  3. Jolley's Ranch
    • Location: Springville, UT
    • Year Established: 2015
    • Special Note: Closed in the winter.
    • See this course on UDisc: Jolley's Ranch
  4. Solitude Mountain Resort
    • Location: Brighton, UT
    • Year Established: 2001
    • Special Note: Closed in the winter.
    • See this course on UDisc: Solitude Mountain Resort
  5. Thunderbird Gardens Disc Golf Course
    • Location: Cedar City, UT
    • Year Established: 2019
    • Special Note: Prepare to hike, excellent desert course.
    • See this course on UDisc: Thunderbird Gardens DGC
